Process

How We Work

An agile, parallel-track way to turn history into a great game - disciplines that run at once and iterate, not a waterfall. Start where there's clarity, prototype with placeholders, and swap in authenticity as research lands.

TL;DR

We don't build this in a straight line. The work splits into parallel tracks - tech, art & sound, design, narrative, and research - that each advance wherever clarity already exists. Tech POCs and art/sound explorations can start now with placeholder assets; historically-specific content slots in later against a stable fact schema. A living decision & dependency tracker keeps the team free to work where they feel inspired while minimising throwaway work. The 98/2 reality-to-convenience rule and the confidence model still govern everything.

Agile, not waterfall

The disciplines further down all have to happen - but not in a fixed order. Treat them as parallel tracks that interleave and iterate, not gates you clear one at a time. The only real constraints are genuine dependencies (you can't finalise the festival calendar before the year is fixed); everything else can move now. Prototype early with placeholders, keep the seams clean, and replace stand-ins with researched detail as it lands - so momentum never waits on perfect information.

Passion-project principle

This is a passion project, and people do their best work where they feel inspired. The method exists to protect that freedom while staying optimal: work any track you're drawn to, as long as you build on settled decisions, use clean placeholders for what's unsettled, and don't create work that a pending decision is likely to throw away. Inspiration first, discipline underneath.

Parallel workstreams

The project runs as several tracks at once. Each advances wherever clarity exists today, and hands off to the others through stable interfaces (the fact schema, the art style guide, the input model).

The rule for starting something

Before opening a new piece of work, ask: does a pending decision change what I'd build? If no - go, inspired and unblocked. If yes - either build the part that won't change, behind a placeholder, or help settle the decision first. That single question is how we stay optimal and avoid throwaway work.

The nine disciplines (run in parallel)

Every discipline below has to happen for the game to be what it claims to be - but they interleave and loop rather than run front to back. Numbered for reference only, not as a running order.

hourglass_empty

1 - Historical snapshot

Fix the era, then one specific year. The era is decided (1850-1900); the exact year collapses ambiguity in the fine detail.

balance

2 - Fantasy level (98/2)

98% historical reality, 2% gameplay convenience. No magic or monsters as real; folklore exists exactly as believed.

menu_book

3 - Knowledge Bible

One source of truth across World, Society, Economy, Government, Architecture, Daily Life, Nature, Language, and Timeline.

travel_explore

4 - Anthropologist's eye

Kings are ~1% of the game. Study how people swept floors, preserved fish, carried water, mourned, and repaired roofs.

library_books

5 - Five kinds of sources

Primary accounts, indigenous sources (incl. the Cheitharol Kumbaba), anthropology, archaeology, living traditions - cross-referenced.

database

6 - Historical Database

Every fact a structured object - description, year range, confidence, sources, evidence, downstream uses. The "world engine."

stadia_controller

7 - Mechanics from history

Ask what actually filled people's days - that list is the systems: rice, weaving, fishing, Lallup, festivals, markets.

favorite

8 - Emotional arc

The reward is belonging and legacy - a respected household, children who inherit it, a name kept in oral tradition.

diversity_3

9 - Advisory circle

Bring historians, archaeologists, linguists, craftspeople and elders in from the start to challenge assumptions early.

The 98 / 2 rule, concretely

Allowed (historical reality)

  • Folklore, dreams, festivals, and beliefs as believed
  • Real crops, tools, foods (ngari), clothing (phanek, pheijom)
  • Real institutions - Lallup service, women-run markets, the king's court
  • Period-accurate architecture, language, and script

Forbidden / convenience-only

  • No magic, no fantasy creatures, no mythical monsters as real
  • Minor time compression for a fun daily loop (the 2%)
  • Gentle UI affordances that never contradict the record
  • No anachronistic crops, tools, or customs "because they're fun"

Every entry in the Historical Database carries these fields. This schema is the contract that Historical Research and Technical Design share.

ConfidenceHow sure we areHigh

Confidence model

A fact's confidence governs how prominently it may drive gameplay. Low-confidence facts can flavour a background, but must never be a load-bearing mechanic.

High Multiple independent sources agree - safe as a core mechanic.

Medium One solid source or strong inference - usable, flagged for review.

Low / Speculative Single weak source or extrapolation - background flavour only, never a core system.

The test that matters

If players finish the game feeling they have lived in historical Manipur - not merely visited a picturesque version of it - the method worked.
