Benchmarking · Synthesis

Lessons for Our Game

What both benchmarks agree on, distilled into portable patterns — the machine we reproduce for 1700s Manipur, with each part's Meitei re-authoring named.

TL;DR

Across Stardew and the Japanese rural-life cluster, the same machine appears: overlapping loops under gentle scarcity, a deep core craft where mastery is the reward, relationships as progression, ambient story that scales, and a cozy, low-stakes contract. We emulate that machine and refill every culture-bound part from the Knowledge Bible. Sakuna is our north star: authenticity is the fun.

The convergent machine

Where two very different games agree, the pattern is probably load-bearing. These are the patterns both share — and the ones we treat as design law.

Overlapping loops under scarcity

Short days nested in seasons and years, with finite time/energy making each morning a small, safe choice. Always something finishing "tomorrow."

A deep core craft

One rich, multi-step activity (farming) where mastery itself is the reward — Sakuna proves depth beats decoration.

Relationships as progression

A living town where friendship is a track with narrative payoffs — hearts, events, marriage, family.

Ambient, scaling story

Story woven into daily life and slow-unfolding NPCs, plus content that keeps opening — no wall, no forced end.

Interlocking systems

Each activity feeds the others (combat↔farming, farming↔cooking↔gifting), so nothing is a dead end.

The cozy contract

Low stakes, soft failure, autonomy, and a beautiful kind world — safety is the product.

Where the two differ — and what we take from each

Stardew gives us

Breadth & polish: many activities, a whole town, a value-laden spine choice, and best-in-class accessibility and pacing.

Sakuna gives us

Depth & authenticity: a research-grade farming sub-game, mastery-as-power, found family, and the proof that specificity is fun.

Our blend

Stardew's welcoming breadth around a Sakuna-deep Meitei paddy core — accessible on the surface, deep for those who dig.

Design pillars this implies

The benchmarks point at a small set of non-negotiables. Proposed for Foundation to ratify as pillars.

  1. Authenticity is the fun

    Specificity, drawn from research, is the feature — not a skin. (Sakuna's proof.)

  2. Cozy, never cruel

    Low stakes, soft failure, autonomy, and a kind world are protected at all costs.

  3. Belonging over accumulation

    The reward is a place in a living community, not just a bigger number.

  4. Deep core, gentle surface

    Accessible to newcomers; endlessly masterable for the devoted.

  5. Everything connects

    No dead-end systems — each activity feeds another, as in both benchmarks.

Where this goes next
