Domain 15

Benchmarking & References

What we can learn from the best cozy life-sims — Stardew Valley and the Japanese rural-life games — so we borrow proven fun without importing someone else's culture.

TL;DR

We study reference titles to steal mechanics and feel, never setting. Stardew Valley sets the bar for the cozy loop and approachability; Japanese rural-life games (Sakuna, Story of Seasons) show how to make farming culturally specific and ritual-rich. Each benchmark page pulls apart what works, what to adapt for 1700s Manipur, and what to deliberately reject.

Borrow the verb, not the world

The trap is copying Stardew's festivals or Sakuna's rice ritual wholesale. We take the underlying mechanic (a seasonal festival; a deep farming sub-game) and rebuild its content entirely from the Knowledge Bible so it is genuinely Meitei.

How we read a benchmark

Every reference page uses the same three-column lens so comparisons stay honest.

Emulate

Proven mechanics and feel that transfer well — the cozy loop, gentle onboarding, meaningful NPCs.

Adapt

Good ideas that must be re-skinned to Meitei reality — festivals, crops, tools, calendar.

Reject

Things that would break authenticity or tone — anachronisms, borrowed folklore, wrong economy.
