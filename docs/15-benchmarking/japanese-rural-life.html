Benchmark · Reference Cluster

Japanese Rural Life

Sakuna: Of Rice and Ruin, Story of Seasons, and kin — the clearest proof that farming can be deeply culturally specific, ritualized, and still joyful. Our closest model for method.

TL;DR

These games make farming mean something by tying it to a real culture's calendar, food, and ritual. Sakuna in particular turns rice cultivation into an authentic, satisfying sub-game. That is exactly the ambition for Meitei paddy life — deep, specific, and reverent — minus any borrowed Japanese content.

The reference cluster

Sakuna: Of Rice and Ruin

Rice farming as a genuine, research-backed craft — the model for making our paddy cycle deep rather than decorative.

Story of Seasons / Harvest Moon

The long-running template for calendar, festivals, town relationships, and seasonal rhythm.

Various rural sims

Kin titles that show ritual, food culture, and slow living as core content, not flavour.

What we take, adapt, and reject

Emulate

Farming as a deep, respectful craft; ritual and food as real systems; a calendar that structures the whole game; reverence for the land.

Adapt

Rice cultivation → Manipur's paddy & terrace methods; Shinto/Buddhist ritual → Meitei practice; Japanese cuisine → chak, ngari, chakhao, local dishes.

Reject

Any Japanese deity, folklore, architecture, or aesthetic. The method transfers; the content must be 100% Meitei.

Why this cluster matters most for method

Sakuna is the proof of concept

Sakuna's team consulted real rice-farming knowledge, and players felt it. It proves our core bet: if we build the Meitei paddy cycle from genuine research, the specificity is the fun. It is the single best argument for the "playable ethnography" approach.
